Refreshing Tax Seminar for Churches, Pastors & Ministries.

Thu, May 21, 20207:00 PM Fri, May 22, 202011:00 AM


Learn More Register

Join us this Spring in beautiful Leavenworth, WA for "What's New" and "In Compliance" for Churches, Pastors, Administrators & Ministries.

We are excited to spend a couple of days with you in Leavenworth this Spring not only to relax and enjoy this great location but also some great information. We'll cover the necessary topics that keep Churches, Pastors, Administrators, and Ministries in compliance. Looking forward to being able to answer questions and bring you a little peace of mind in the area of "Taxes and The Church / Ministries"

Hot Topics...

  • What's new with the IRS ? - What you really do need to know and why
  • Tax Reporting - Everything you should know but are afraid to ask.
  • Payroll & Employee - Should your staff be employees or independent contractors. What's the difference and who gets to choose?
  • Accounting - Financials, Funds, Audits...Are we having fun yet?
  • Policies and Procedures - When can I throw out old records... after 50 years? What do I have to keep?
  • Housing - What's new and what should I know.

Who Should Attend: The knowledge gained in this seminar will be a great benefit to Bookkeepers, Treasurers, Pastors, Executive Directors, Board Members and Administrators. Something for everyone!

We will provide for you:

  • Special Guest Speaker – Amy Artiga, the writer of “The Pastor’s Wallet”
  • Seminar Manual / Pen
  • Lunch on Thursday
  • Coffee & Refreshments through the day
  • Friendly & helpful staff

The The Seminar will be presented by:

Wayne E. Vinson, CPA, PS who is a CPA with almost 30 years of experience in tax and accounting. He has served on numerous ministry boards, as an elder in his church, is an ordained minister and owns his own CPA firm in Spokane, Washington. Currently serving over 700 tax clients of which more than 100 are pastors or ministers! His firm provides consulting and accounting services to a number of churches and ministries including annual church audits. He has worked in Children's Ministries for more than 35 years and has a way of making complex issues more meaningful and simplified. He speaks in a language that can be clearly understood. He and his wife Annie of 40 years, have 3 children and 14 grandchildren.

Testimonial: “Taxes are a big deal and it’s hard to find a CPA or tax professional who actually understands the intricacies of how they apply to pastors. ….(Wayne) is well versed in taxes and how they apply to both pastors and churches.” - Amy – Writer of “The Pastor’s Wallet”
